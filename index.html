What is water quality management?


Water quality management is the monitoring, controlling, and improvement of water-related processes to ensure that they meet their most urgent use for humans as well as maintaining ecological ecosystems. These include various activities directed at protecting rivers, lakes, groundwater, and coastal areas against pollution, over-extraction, or contamination.

Purpose of water quality management:-


  • Working to keep water safe for human use.
  • Managing water resources to ensure availability for present and future generations.
  • Good water quality protects industries such as fisheries, agriculture, and tourism.
  • Proper water management prevents floods by preserving wetlands and natural filters.
  • Sustainable water management strengthens ecosystems and communities' ability to adapt to climate change.

Process of water quality management


  • Collect and analyze water samples.
  • Define water quality rules based on use (drinking, recreation, etc.).
  • Find pollution sources like factories or farms.
  • Evaluate the impact on health and the environment.
  • Apply measures to reduce pollution and restore water.
  • Educate and involve the community.
  • Regularly check water to ensure quality.
  • Share results and ensure laws are followed.
